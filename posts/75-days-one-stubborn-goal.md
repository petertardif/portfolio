Seventy-five days, no exceptions, no substitutions. Two workouts, a strict diet, a gallon of water, a progress photo, ten pages of reading. Miss one requirement on any day and you start over at day one.

## Why I did it

Not for the physique. I did it because I wanted to know if I could keep a promise to myself when literally no one else was checking. Coaching, teaching, managing — all of it involves holding other people to standards. I wanted to hold myself to one, in public, with a clock running.

## What actually got hard

It wasn't the workouts. It was day 40-something, nothing going wrong, just tired of the routine, tempted to let one thing slide because "it's just one day." That's the whole game. The challenge isn't physical — it's whether you'll lower the bar when no one's watching.

## What carried over

I finished. But the real payoff showed up afterward, in smaller commitments — shipping the thing I said I'd ship by Friday, showing up to the thing I said I'd show up to, even on the days I didn't feel like it. Discipline turned out to be a skill, not a mood. You can practice it on purpose.
