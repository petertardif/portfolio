This is the running log I keep meaning to formalize: the repairs I've taken on myself instead of calling someone, in the order I remember them.

## The wins

The garbage disposal that just needed the reset button (felt like a genius, was not). The toilet flapper that turned an $80 plumber visit into a $6 part and fifteen minutes. Re-caulking the shower, badly the first time, correctly the second time, which is basically the whole theme of this list.

## The failures

I once "fixed" a leaking pipe joint with plumber's tape when it needed an actual new fitting. It held for four days. I've also learned, the hard way, that YouTube tutorials skip the part where the previous owner did something unconventional that makes your situation not match the video at all.

## Why I keep doing it anyway

It's not really about the money, though that helps. It's the same instinct that got me into development: I want to understand how the thing works, not just have it work. A failed repair still teaches me something a phone call to a professional never will.

## The actual rule I follow now

If it's water, gas, or structural, I at least call someone to sanity-check my plan before I touch it. Everything else is fair game, and worst case, I've bought myself an interesting story and a trip to the hardware store.
