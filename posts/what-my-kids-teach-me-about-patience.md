No amount of managing statewide programs prepared me for negotiating a shoe-wearing dispute with a three-year-old at 7:12am when we need to leave at 7:15.

## The lesson I keep relearning

Patience isn't the absence of frustration. It's choosing the response anyway, while the frustration is still right there. I used to think patient people just didn't feel the urge to snap. They do. They just have a longer pause between the urge and the reaction.

## A short list of things my kids have taught me this year

- Explaining "why" actually works, even on a three-year-old, even when you're in a hurry.
- The tantrum is rarely about the thing it looks like it's about.
- "Because I said so" ends the argument but doesn't end the lesson.
- Watching them fail at something small and try again, unprompted, is the best reminder that resilience is trainable, not innate.

## Why it matters at work too

Every one of those lessons applies directly to leading adults. People are rarely upset about the thing they say they're upset about. Explaining "why" beats "because I said so" in a standup just as much as it does at the breakfast table.
