Most people's experience with "custom software" involves a sales call, a project manager, a developer they never talk to, and a invoice that doesn't match what they thought they were buying. PJT Development exists because I got tired of watching that happen to people.

## No handoffs

I write the code. I'm also the one who explains it to you. There's no game of telephone between what you asked for and what gets built, because there's only one person in the loop.

## No jargon, on purpose

If I can't explain what I'm building and why in plain English, I don't understand it well enough yet to build it correctly. That's not a communication tactic — it's a forcing function. Jargon is often a way of hiding that nobody's checked whether the plan actually makes sense.

## What that looks like in practice

- You get a plain-language explanation of tradeoffs before I write a line of code, not after.
- Timelines are honest, including the parts where I say "this will take longer than you want."
- You can reach me directly. There's no support ticket between you and the person who can actually fix the problem.

## The tradeoff, honestly

I can't take on everything at once, and there's a ceiling to how fast I can move compared to a team. I think that's a fair trade for someone who explains every step and never disappears behind a handoff.
